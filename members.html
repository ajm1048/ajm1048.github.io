Jim Morrison

"Light My Fire" By The Doorsr

Jim Morrison was born on December 8th, 1944. He was a singer, song writer, and poet. He was the lead singer of the band The Doors. Their most popular songs were “Riders On the Storm” “Light My Fire” and “Love Me Two Times. Jim Morrison died on July 3rd, 1971 in Paris, France. The Cause of death was heart failure, although no autopsy was done because it was not required in France.

Janis Joplin

"Summertime"

Janis Joplin was born January 19, 1943. She was potentially the biggest female rock star of her time in the 60s. Her music was mainly blues influenced rock. Some of her popular songs include, “Summertime”, “Cheap Thrills”, and “piece of my heart”. On October 4th, 1970 Janis Joplin was found dead in her hotel room. The cause of death was an accidental heroin overdose. She died at the age of 27.

Brian Jones

"Beast of Burden" by The Rolling Stones

Brian Jones was born on February 28th, 1942 in Cheltenham, England. He was the founder of the UK rock band The Rolling Stones. The band had blues inspired rock and roll, and were famous during the 60s. Some of the bands popular songs were, “Beast of Burden”, “Wild Horses”, and “Paint it Black”. Brian Jones died on July 3rd, 1969. He died at the age of 27 by drowning in his pool while intoxicated with drugs and alcohol.
